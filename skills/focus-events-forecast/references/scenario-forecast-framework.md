# Scenario Forecast Framework

Use this reference when a forecast worker needs a compact rubric for building defensible multi-horizon scenarios from a focus log.

## Core Method

1. Start from the source log, not from a blank page.
Extract the current situation, dominant narrative, active catalysts, evidence window, and the main uncertainty already identified in the log.

2. Separate facts from inference.
Treat the source log's dated evidence as the factual baseline. Treat any future path, impact chain, or second-order effect as inference.

3. Build scenarios horizon by horizon.
Do not force the same scenario tree across every horizon. Near-term horizons should focus on immediate catalysts and continuation risk. Longer horizons should focus on follow-through, second-order effects, institutional response, adoption, or regime change.

4. Keep scenarios mutually exclusive within a horizon.
For each horizon, create a small set of distinct outcomes that compete for the same probability budget. The probabilities inside one horizon should sum to `100%`.

## Horizon Guidance

### 1 week

Focus on immediate catalysts, headline risk, implementation steps, price confirmation, official responses, or event resolution that could happen quickly.

Questions to ask:

- Does the current focus persist for another week?
- What is the next scheduled catalyst?
- What can be confirmed or disproved quickly?

### 1 month

Focus on the first full round of follow-through, such as policy messaging, earnings revisions, coalition behavior, customer response, supply adjustments, or narrative rotation.

Questions to ask:

- Does the initial narrative broaden or fade?
- What second-order effects show up after a few weeks?
- Which actors have time to respond?

### 3 months

Focus on implementation, trend establishment, electoral or legislative consequences, capex shifts, adoption curves, or sustained market leadership changes.

Questions to ask:

- Does the situation settle into a durable pattern?
- Which delayed effects become visible?
- What would change medium-term expectations materially?

### 1 year

Focus on structural outcomes, institutional changes, regime shifts, competitive re-ranking, or lasting geopolitical, scientific, or market consequences.

Questions to ask:

- What longer-lasting state could emerge from today's focus?
- Which outcomes would still matter after the immediate narrative is gone?
- What would count as a genuinely different regime one year out?

## Probability Rules

- Use `2` to `4` scenarios per horizon.
- Make the probabilities sum to `100%` within each horizon.
- Prefer `5%` or `10%` increments unless the evidence clearly supports a finer split.
- Avoid `0%` and `100%` unless the outcome is already effectively resolved.
- Do not copy the same exact percentage pattern across every horizon without justification.

## Scenario Anatomy

Each scenario should include:

- a short, specific label
- a probability
- a concise narrative of what happens
- the reasoning that links the current situation to that path
- the signposts that would increase or decrease confidence in that path

## Quality Checks

Before finalizing a forecast, check:

- Are the scenarios genuinely different from one another?
- Does every scenario trace back to something present in the source log?
- Are probabilities sensible rather than decorative?
- Does each horizon reflect the time available for change?
- Is the lead scenario clear?

## Domain Adaptation

### Market logs

Translate focus themes into event paths such as policy repricing, earnings confirmation, positioning squeeze, demand slowdown, supply shock, or sector rotation.

### Political logs

Translate focus themes into event paths such as passage failure, court intervention, implementation delays, coalition fracture, polling shifts, escalation, or negotiated settlement.

### Tech logs

Translate focus themes into event paths such as launch success, regulatory pushback, customer adoption, outage fallout, pricing pressure, or capital-spending acceleration.

### Science logs

Translate focus themes into event paths such as replication success, mission milestone, regulatory milestone, funding continuation, trial setback, or faster-than-expected translation into downstream use.
