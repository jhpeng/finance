# Tech Evidence Framework

Use this reference when the request needs a more rigorous technology-attention workflow, a scoring rubric, or a reusable output template.

## Source Priority

Use the strongest source that fits the claim.

- Product launches and platform changes: prefer official blogs, docs, release notes, changelogs, keynote materials, and pricing pages.
- Outages, incidents, and security issues: prefer vendor status pages, incident writeups, security advisories, CERT or CISA bulletins, and customer notices.
- Financial and corporate catalysts: prefer investor-relations pages, earnings decks, transcripts, filings, acquisition announcements, and company guidance.
- Policy and legal moves: prefer official agency releases, legislative texts, court filings, standards bodies, and consultation papers.
- Narrative confirmation: use high-quality tech or business reporting to see whether a topic is recurring across coverage rather than appearing as a one-off headline.

## Theme Scoring

Score each candidate theme from 0 to 3 in each category.

- Coverage breadth: how many independent, recent sources mention the same theme.
- Primary-source confirmation: how directly the claim is supported by official materials.
- Ecosystem reach: how many companies, developers, users, or enterprise buyers are affected.
- Catalyst proximity: whether a launch, earnings event, conference, ruling, outage, or security disclosure is imminent or still unfolding.

Interpret the total score this way:

- 9 to 12: dominant technology focus.
- 6 to 8: active but secondary theme.
- 3 to 5: watchlist item or emerging narrative.
- 0 to 2: weak evidence or noise.

## Synthesis Checklist

Before finalizing the answer, confirm all of the following:

- The top themes are supported by more than one recent signal, or by one authoritative source with clear downstream impact.
- The dates in the response are explicit.
- Each theme has a clear why-now explanation.
- The answer separates announcement, availability, adoption, and business impact.
- The affected companies, platforms, or users are named directly.
- Upcoming catalysts are named when they matter.

## Suggested Response Template

Use or adapt this structure:

1. `Focus Now`
Name the top 3 to 5 technology themes in rank order.

2. `Theme Breakdown`
For each theme, explain the catalyst, affected companies or platforms, business transmission path, and evidence.

3. `Ecosystem Read`
Summarize what developers, enterprises, consumers, and infrastructure providers are signaling together or where they disagree.

4. `What To Watch Next`
List the next launches, earnings calls, conferences, policy deadlines, or security disclosures that could rotate attention.

5. `Confidence And Gaps`
State where the evidence is strong, mixed, or incomplete.
