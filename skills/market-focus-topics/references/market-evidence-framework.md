# Market Evidence Framework

Use this reference when the request needs a more rigorous market-attention workflow, a scoring rubric, or a reusable output template.

## Source Priority

Use the strongest source that fits the claim.

- Market data: use live or recent price data to confirm whether a theme is visible in indexes, yields, FX, commodities, or single names.
- Macro and policy: prefer central bank sites, statistical agencies, finance ministries, Treasury releases, and official calendars.
- Company catalysts: prefer investor-relations pages, earnings decks, transcripts, SEC filings, and company guidance.
- Regulation and geopolitics: prefer official government or agency statements first, then reputable financial reporting for market interpretation.
- Narrative confirmation: use high-quality financial reporting to see whether a topic is recurring across coverage rather than appearing as a one-off headline.

## Theme Scoring

Score each candidate theme from 0 to 3 in each category.

- Coverage breadth: how many independent, recent sources mention the same theme.
- Price confirmation: how clearly the relevant assets are reacting in the same direction or with unusual volatility.
- Cross-asset spillover: whether the theme affects more than one market or sector.
- Catalyst proximity: whether a release, meeting, earnings event, or policy action is imminent.

Interpret the total score this way:

- 9 to 12: dominant market focus.
- 6 to 8: active but secondary theme.
- 3 to 5: watchlist item or emerging narrative.
- 0 to 2: weak evidence or noise.

## Synthesis Checklist

Before finalizing the answer, confirm all of the following:

- The top themes are supported by more than one recent signal.
- The dates in the response are explicit.
- Each theme has a clear why-now explanation.
- Asset impact is described, not just the headline.
- The answer separates confirmed evidence from inference.
- Upcoming catalysts are named when they matter.

## Suggested Response Template

Use or adapt this structure:

1. `Focus Now`
Name the top 3 to 5 themes in rank order.

2. `Theme Breakdown`
For each theme, explain the catalyst, affected assets, market transmission path, and evidence.

3. `Cross-Market Read`
Summarize what rates, equities, FX, and commodities are signaling together or where they disagree.

4. `What To Watch Next`
List the next releases, earnings calls, auctions, speeches, or deadlines that could rotate attention.

5. `Confidence And Gaps`
State where the evidence is strong, mixed, or incomplete.
